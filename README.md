In Motion was made to help people track their weight without all the complicated stuff getting in the way. A lot of fitness apps try to do too much, so I just focused on making something simple that people would actually want to use. The app needed a login screen so your data stays private, a place to see all your weights and add new ones, and the ability to delete stuff if you needed to. I kept everything really simple and clean because nobody wants to figure out confusing buttons or menus. That's basically why the design worked, you know. For coding, I learned that naming things the same way everywhere and staying organized from the beginning is way better than trying to fix stuff later. That's something I'm definitely doing again on my next project because it saves so much time. Testing by compiling my code showed me how important every little detail is, especially with the textID stuff that kept giving me problems. One wrong name and everything breaks, which is why that process matters so much. The biggest challenge was dealing with all those naming issues, and I figured out that making a list of all your IDs before you start coding saves you from a ton of headaches. The DatabaseHelper class was probably what I did best with because it actually works and connects everything properly, and that felt really good to get right.
